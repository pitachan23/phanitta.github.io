Senior EE Capstone Course
Medical Image Recognition for Arthroscopic Images. (Sponsored by Arthrex) [Paper] [Poster] [Project Page]
We built a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in Tensorflow to detect and classify the types of instruments used in the arthroscopy or "keyhole surgery." Due to homogeneity of the tissues and organs shown in the arthroscopic videos, a tool classification algorithm provides a way of summarizing videos by linking specific functionalities of each tool with its relevant events. Our goal is to lessen the workload of surgeons in video annotation, which can be very time-consuming. Our project has achieved 85% classification accuracy. Our team was also selected to present our project at the Engineering Design Expo, 2018 at UCSB.

Neural Networks and Deep Learning Course
Image Colorization using Generative Adversarial Networks. [Paper] [Code]
We implemented a Conditional Generative Adversarial Network (cGAN) based on pix2pix by Isola et al. to colorize high-resolution grayscale images. We used 459 pairs (grayscale and color) of 2048x4096 landscape images as our training set, and 20 grayscale images as our testing set. Our results showed that the cGAN is able to color grayscale images with reasonable reality. To further expand its application, we constructed stitch-and-average strategy to colorize images with very high resolution (greater than 2048x4096).

Function Approximation using Neural Network [Report] [Code]
I modeled a two-layer neural network that generates a square pulse, given an 8-bit binary input. Each square pulse was then multiplied by a height, which was approximated from a graph of an unknown function. The sum of all square pulses was used to plot the unknown function.
